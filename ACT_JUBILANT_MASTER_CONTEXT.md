## Executive Summary

ACT Jubilant is not a general NDIS provider.

ACT Jubilant is a boutique, relationship-led disability support provider focused on helping participants build meaningful daily lives through structured, interest-based engagement.

The organisation exists to address:
- participant boredom
- lack of routine
- lack of meaningful engagement
- inconsistent support relationships

Core belief:

> Support should not simply fill time.
> Support should create meaningful daily life.

# Brand Identity

## Purpose

We exist to help people with disability feel capable, engaged, and proud of what they create and achieve.

## Vision

A local ACT community where people with disability are recognised for their interests, skills, and contributions, not defined by limitation.

## Mission

We provide consistent, relationship-based in-home and community support in the ACT, designing structured programs around participant interests to build independence, confidence, and belonging.

# Core Narrative

## Problem

Many participants experience:

- boredom
- isolation
- passive routines
- limited engagement
- inconsistent support workers

Support often becomes:
- transport
- appointments
- personal care
- supervision

without meaningful engagement.

## Approach

ACT Jubilant structures support around:

- hobbies
- interests
- routine
- community participation
- meaningful activities

Workers do not simply accompany participants.

Workers actively engage participants in activities that build confidence, routine, identity and connection.

## Outcome

Participants experience:

- confidence
- engagement
- routine
- social connection
- meaningful daily life

Families gain confidence that participants are living active lives rather than simply passing time.

# Operational Proof Points

## Participant-Driven Activities

Activities evolve from participant interests.

Examples:
- Gardening
- Woodworking
- Community outings
- Crosswords
- Hobby development

## Participant-Led Worker Matching

Participants help select workers.

Process:
- Multiple introductions
- Trial shifts
- Participant selection
- Stable team creation

## Consistency of Workers

Worker continuity is intentionally prioritised.

Benefits:
- Trust
- Stability
- Predictability
- Strong relationships

## Founder-Led Onboarding

Manish spends time understanding:
- personality
- preferences
- communication style
- routines
- goals

before worker assignment.

## Flexible Activity-Based Shifts

Support is activity-led, not task-list-led.

Examples:
- Social outings
- Hobbies
- Community events
- Meeting friends

## Coordinator Communication

Regular:
- updates
- observations
- feedback
- communication

## Therapy Reinforcement

Recommendations from therapists are embedded into daily activities.

Examples:

Gardening → mobility

Cooking → independence

Woodworking → dexterity

Walking → endurance

## Small Group Programs

Target size:
- ~6 participants maximum

Examples:
- Art groups
- Cooking sessions
- Hobby workshops
