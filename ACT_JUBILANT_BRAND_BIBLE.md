# EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

ACT Jubilant is a boutique disability support provider based in the Australian Capital Territory.

The organisation is intentionally designed around participant engagement, meaningful routine, hobbies, interests, and relationship-based support.

The company is not attempting to become the largest provider.

Its goal is to become the most trusted provider for participants who benefit from consistency, engagement, creativity, routine, and community participation.

Core positioning:

SUPPORT SHOULD NOT SIMPLY FILL TIME.
SUPPORT SHOULD CREATE MEANINGFUL DAILY LIFE.

This statement should influence every page, message, advertisement, referral conversation, social media post, and participant interaction.

- Limited stimulation

- Supervised
- Processed

- Valued

ACT Jubilant addresses all three.

## STRATEGIC FRAMING

The homepage is a **persuasion document**, not a business plan.

**Primary conversion:** Make a Referral (support coordinators, therapists)  
**Secondary conversion:** Book a Consultation (families, self-referrers)  
**Tertiary outcome:** Credibility for visitors who save and return later

**Narrative spine:** Problem → Approach → Outcome

**Psychological flow (Sections 5–7):** Imagine → Fit → Prove

**Governing question:** What must the visitor *believe* after each section — before any CTA?

**Identity thread (woven through Sections 3, 4, 5, 7):**  
Support should help people build confidence, interests, and a **sense of identity** — recognised for what they build, learn, and contribute. Not just occupied. Not just supervised. *Seen.*

**What makes this homepage unusual:**  
Most provider sites run Hero → Services → Why Choose Us.  
ACT Jubilant runs Hero → Problem → Philosophy → Proof of differentiation.  
Services are secondary; belief comes first.

---

## GLOBAL RULES

1. Each section must be **understandable within 15 seconds** of scanning.
2. Preserve depth through **links to dedicated pages**, not long homepage explanations.
3. The homepage must make someone **believe something** — not just describe services.
4. Section 4 is a **differentiation section**, not a feature section. Every proof card must answer: *"Why is this different from a typical provider?"*
5. **Never use in hero or above-the-fold copy:** leading, 24/7, comprehensive, quality care, compassionate support, caring, flexible (as differentiators), NDIS services list.
6. Do not blame support or care. The issue is **care without engagement** — not care itself.
7. **Hero is not the activity. The hero is the process.** (Reinforced in Section 4 intro.)

## SECTION 4 — DESIGN DECISION (APPROVED)

**Format:** Proof cards in 3 groups — **not** a process diagram.

**Why not a process diagram:** Visitors are asking *"Why should I choose you?"* not *"How does onboarding work?"* A process diagram hides differentiators.

**Why proof cards:** Each card communicates one belief instantly. Scannable in ~5 seconds per group.

**Three groups:**

| Group | Belief created | Cards |
|-------|----------------|-------|
| Participant Experience | "This feels personal." | Participant-led matching, Consistent support teams, Participant-driven activities |
| Professional Confidence | "This works alongside my plan and therapy." | Therapy reinforcement, Coordinator communication |
| Boutique Difference | "This isn't a high-volume provider." | Founder-led onboarding, Small structured groups |

**Design priority:** Section 4 deserves the most visual weight on the homepage after the hero. Not 7 identical cards — group headers visible on scan.
